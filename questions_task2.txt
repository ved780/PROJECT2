Interview Questions and Answers - Phishing Awareness

1. What is phishing?
Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers trick people into revealing sensitive information (like passwords or credit card details) by pretending to be a trusted source, usually through fake emails or websites.

2. How to identify a phishing email?
- Suspicious or misspelled sender address
- Urgent or threatening language ("Act now!")
- Mismatched or fake links (hover to inspect)
- Unexpected attachments
- Poor grammar or generic greetings ("Dear user")
- Requests for personal or financial info

3. What is email spoofing?
Email spoofing is when an attacker forges the sender's address to make the email look like it's from someone trusted (e.g., a company or a colleague), even though it’s not.

4. Why are phishing emails dangerous?
They can:
- Steal personal or financial data
- Spread malware or ransomware
- Lead to identity theft
- Compromise accounts or systems

5. How can you verify the sender’s authenticity?
- Check the sender’s full email address (not just the name)
- Use “Show original” or header analysis to confirm the source
- Look for domain mismatches or authentication failures (SPF/DKIM)
- Contact the sender through official channels if unsure

6. What tools can analyze email headers?
- MXToolbox
- Google Admin Toolbox
- Mailheader.org
- Email clients with advanced header view (like Outlook or Thunderbird)

7. What actions should be taken on suspected phishing emails?
- Do not click links or download attachments
- Report it (to IT, Google, Outlook, etc.)
- Mark as spam/phishing
- Delete it
- If clicked, change passwords and run antivirus scan

8. How do attackers use social engineering in phishing?
They exploit human psychology — urgency, fear, trust — to trick users into clicking, downloading, or entering credentials. Common tactics include:
- Fake account suspension warnings
- Fake invoices
- Fake tech support or HR emails
